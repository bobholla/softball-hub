How to Maximize Every Camp

Before the Camp

  • Email the coaching staff 1 week before (use the camp template from Coach Contact page)
  • Research the coaches — know their names and what they coach
  • Pack: full catching gear, bat, cleats, batting gloves, extra hair ties, water, snacks
  • Get a good night's sleep. Eat a proper breakfast.
  • Print 10-15 copies of your player profile

During the Camp

  • Arrive 15-20 min early. First impression matters.
  • Introduce yourself: "Hi Coach [Name], I'm Charlie Holladay, I emailed you about attending today."
  • Listen and implement feedback IMMEDIATELY. Coaches watch coachability.
  • Go 100% on every rep. Even warmups. Coaches are ALWAYS watching.
  • Be vocal. As a catcher — communicate, call balls, direct traffic, lead.
  • Cheer for others. Be a great teammate. Energy and attitude matter.

After the Camp

  • Thank every coach personally before leaving. Handshake, eye contact.
  • Send a thank-you email within 24 hours.
  • Write down feedback — what did coaches say? What to work on?
  • Post on social media: "Great day at [School] camp! Thank you @[handle]!"

What Coaches Evaluate at Camps

  • Coachability — Do you listen and adjust? (#1 factor)
  • Attitude/Energy — Positive, competitive, team-first
  • Mechanics — Catching: receiving, framing, blocking, footwork
  • Arm strength — Pop time, throw accuracy
  • Bat — Swing mechanics, approach, bat speed
  • Speed — Home-to-first, baserunning instincts
  • Recovery — How you handle mistakes. Shake it off or dwell?

Types of Camps — Know the Difference

Camp Type What It Is Cost Recruiting Value
College Prospect/Elite Camp Hosted by a college's coaching staff ON their campus. Skills stations + evaluation. This is the gold standard. $75-300 HIGHEST — Basically an audition.
Position-Specific Camp (P/C) Focused on catchers (and pitchers). Smaller group, more individual attention on position skills. $100-250 HIGHEST for catchers — Coaches evaluate catching skills up close.
Multi-Day Summer Camp 3-4 day camp. More instruction, more reps, deeper evaluation. Alabama's is this type. $300-500 Very High — Extended time = coaches really get to know you.
Skills Clinic Shorter (2-3 hrs). Focused on instruction. Less formal evaluation. $50-150 Medium — Great for improvement + coaches still notice standouts.
Third-Party Showcase Run by orgs like Softball Factory, NCSA. College coaches may or may not attend. $200-500+ Varies — Research coach attendance before paying.

Power Primary Goal
Program Context

13-year-old catcher preparing for 14U PGF travel ball (May 2026). Currently in ulnar nerve recovery with modified grip. Expanding from 1x/week hitting to a full 3-day power hitting development system.

This program integrates with the Nerve Recovery Program — hitting days are placed on Tuesday, Thursday, and Friday to complement recovery training.

Why This Schedule Works
  • Tuesday — Core/rotation primes hip connection for tee work
  • Thursday — Power/plyo primes explosiveness for machine BP
  • Friday — Dedicated power hitting day (replaces old Friday hitting + core)
Nerve Recovery Rules — Every Session
  • Modified grip: One-finger-off (pinky off bat) or extra padding on handle
  • NO check swings — commit fully or take the pitch
  • STOP immediately if numbness, tingling, or burning occurs
  • If symptoms flare during machine BP, switch to tracking-only (watch pitches without swinging)
  • Quality over quantity — stop if mechanics break down

Pick ONE mechanical cue per session and own it. Examples: "hips fire first," "hands to the ball," "stay through it," "back elbow slots."

Tracking-Only Fallback

If nerve symptoms flare during machine BP, immediately switch to tracking only:

  • Stand in batter's box with bat on shoulder
  • Call out pitch type ("fastball," "change," "curve") as early as possible
  • Call location ("in," "out," "up," "down")
  • This builds pitch recognition without stressing the nerve

Session total: ~80-90 swings (36 front toss + 44-52 machine)

Visualization

Close eyes. Visualize 3 perfect at-bats: see the pitch, feel the swing, watch the ball fly. Picture the crowd, the pressure, the confidence. 2 minutes of focused mental rehearsal.

Session total: ~60-70 swings (6 overload + 5 regular + 10 underload + 8 regular + 25 situations)

Phase 2: Full Grip (Post-Clearance)

Advancement Gate — All Must Be Met
  • Zero numbness/tingling for 2+ consecutive weeks
  • Normal grip strength during daily activities
  • Pain-free stress ball squeezes
  • Cleared by parent/coach (doctor if symptoms persisted past 4 weeks)

If not met: Continue Phase 1 until all criteria are satisfied.

Same 3-day structure, increased volumes. Weekly total: ~255-280 swings

Count At-Bats

Start each at-bat with a count (0-0, 1-0, 0-1, 2-1, etc.). Adjust approach accordingly:

  • Hitter's count (2-0, 3-1): Hunt YOUR pitch, be aggressive
  • Even count (1-1, 2-2): Balanced approach, ready to react
  • Pitcher's count (0-2, 1-2): Expand zone slightly, protect, put ball in play

Session total: ~100-110 swings

Pre-Session Checklist

  1. Nerve check — any numbness or tingling? If yes, modify or skip
  2. Modified grip setup (Phase 1) or full grip check (Phase 2)
  3. Choose ONE mechanical cue for the session
  4. Equipment ready: game bat, overload bat, underload bat, tee, balls
  5. Hydration — water bottle at station
  6. Film 2-3 swings per session for video review

Post-Session Log

Track after each session:

  • Total swings taken
  • Mechanical cue focus
  • Quality rating (1-10)
  • Any nerve symptoms? (Y/N, describe)
  • Best exit velo (if testing)
  • Notes / what to work on next
